◈ SONIC-VISUAL SYNC — Guide

An engine that cuts your video clips to the music, automatically and on your cue.

1 · Load media

Drop a music track (MP3/WAV) and at least 2 video clips (MP4/WebM). You can drag files, click a drop zone, or use Import a whole folder — every supported video under the folder loads, skipping files above the size limit. Each clip is auto-analyzed for motion, brightness and saturation.

2 · Tag your clips (optional but powerful)

Give each clip up to 3 tags (#Bright, #Fast, #Calm …). Tags steer which clip is chosen for each musical moment via the affinity table. Untagged clips still play — they're matched purely on their auto-features.

3 · Direct the cuts — markers matter

The engine follows one rule: Manual > Auto. Between your markers it auto-cuts on the beat grid every 4–8 beats. For cuts that land on drops, builds and hits, add markers: pick a marker tag, then Shift-click or double-click the waveform at that instant. Drag a flag to move it, right-click to delete. Editing recompiles the timeline live.

If auto-cuts feel off-beat, check the BPM readout and whether the grey beat-grid ticks on the waveform line up with the music. That tells you if the tempo analysis matched your track.

4 · Preview & export

Space plays/pauses. Clips are letterboxed (never cropped or stretched), each starting from its beginning at every cut. GENERATE renders a deterministic frame-by-frame MP4 (H.264 + AAC) and lets you choose where to save it. If the MP4 encoder can't load (e.g. opened via file://), it falls back to a WebM export automatically.
